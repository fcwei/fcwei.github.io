About

I am a PhD candidate in the Yau Mathematical Sciences Center and the Department of Mathematical Sciences at Tsinghua University. My current work focuses on quantum information theory, with interests spanning quantum error correction, quantum algorithms, noise effects, and nonstabilizerness / quantum magic.

Research

A concise snapshot of the themes currently represented on this page. Edit or expand these paragraphs however you like.

Quantum information and computation

I am broadly interested in theoretical quantum information, especially the structure and capabilities of noisy quantum systems, classical simulation of quantum processes, and resource-theoretic aspects of quantum computation.

Quantum error correction and magic

My recent work also explores quantum error correction and long-range nonstabilizerness, aiming to understand how magic, topology, complexity, and fault tolerance interact in many-body and coding-theoretic settings.
